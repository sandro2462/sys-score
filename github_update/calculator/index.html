EndoSysScore (ESS) is a stacking ensemble developed on the GIROC Italian multicentre registry (2010–2023; derivation cohort n=4,334; 21 centres; 30-day mortality 10.6%). Rare high-lethality phenotypes were identified in the derivation cohort by systematic multivariable pattern analysis and used to guide conditional synthetic data augmentation (CTGAN + TIMA clinician-supervised plausibility filtering). The final model combines three base learners (XGBoost shallow, XGBoost moderate, logistic regression) through a LightGBM meta-learner, with isotonic and beta calibration applied on real development data only.

Model performance was evaluated in an independent external validation cohort of 881 patients from three centres (30-day mortality 11.8%). Two of three centres (10 and 23) were entirely absent from the derivation data; a third centre (15) contributed 11 patients to derivation and 320 to validation, with no individual patient overlap confirmed by record linkage. No validation-cohort patient was used for model training, synthetic augmentation, calibration, or phenotype optimisation.

Phenotype transparency note: during phenotype definition, candidate phenotypes were required to demonstrate non-zero representation in the validation cohort to confirm applicability to rare IE presentations. This feasibility check verified representation only; phenotype definitions were fixed before any outcome data from the validation cohort were examined. Full methodology, reproducible code, and de-identified data are openly deposited on Zenodo and GitHub.

How to use EndoSysScore

Clinical guide · ESS v3 · For research use only
  1. Enter patient data using the toggles and numeric fields. All variables refer to the pre-operative assessment.
  2. Watch the phenotype buttons (A–F) change colour in real time: green = no criteria met; orange = partial; light red = almost complete; red fire = all criteria met → phenotype active.
  3. Press "Compute risk" to obtain the 30-day operative mortality from the EndoSysScore model (LightGBM meta-learner, trained on the GIROC 24-centre registry).
  4. Interpret the result: Standard risk (<20%) — routine pathway. High risk (20–40%) — discuss with Heart Team. Very high risk (>40%) — formal multidisciplinary review, informed consent counselling, consider timing and centre volume.
  5. Save results with the Save button — downloads a structured text file with all inputs, active phenotypes, and the risk estimate.
  6. Limitations: ESS was developed and internally confirmed on Italian cardiac surgery centres (2010–2023). External prospective validation is ongoing. It is not a CE-marked or FDA-cleared medical device and must not replace clinical judgment.

How to use the calculator — 5 steps
1
Enter patient data
Use the toggles and numeric fields. All variables refer to the pre-operative assessment. Age and LVEF are entered as numbers; all other variables as on/off toggles.
2
Watch phenotype buttons A–F
Buttons change colour in real time as you enter data. ● Green = no criteria met. ● Orange = partial match. ● Light red = almost complete. ● Red fire = all criteria met → phenotype active.
3
Press "Compute risk"
The EndoSysScore model returns the estimated 30-day operative mortality. The model was trained on 4,334 real patients from 21 GIROC centres with synthetic augmentation of rare phenotypes.
4
Interpret the result
See risk categories below. If a phenotype is active, the button shows which criteria are met and the observed mortality in the validation cohort.
5
Save results
Press ↓ Save to download a structured text file with all inputs, active phenotypes, risk estimate, and date — suitable for clinical records or audit.

Limitations — read before use
⚠ Developed on Italian centres (2010–2023); generalisability to other healthcare systems requires prospective validation.
⚠ Per-phenotype subgroups are small (n=11–35); confidence intervals are wide and should be interpreted directionally.

⚠ External prospective validation is ongoing. Results in this tool reflect the internal confirmation cohort (n=881).
